Nook · v1.0.0 for macOS

A todo list that lives in the corner of your screen.

Nook stays out of your way until you need it. Slam your mouse into a screen corner — your tasks slide in. Done with them? Push them back.

Download for macOS View on GitHub
The Mechanic

Designed around the corner.

Most apps live in your dock or menu bar. Nook lives in the corner of your screen — invisible until you push your mouse there. No keyboard shortcut to remember. No app to launch. Just a corner.

See Nook in motion

Small. Native. Out of the way.

Nook v1.0 · Main panel

A whole day, at a glance.

Nine tasks, four tags, completed ones tucked below — this is what Nook looks like during your day.

Priority flags, due dates, sub-task progress: information density tuned just right. Overdue items turn red so you don't miss them.

Nook v1.0 · Task detail

Has what you need. Doesn't demand it.

Title, description, due date, sub-tasks, multi-tag — every field a real todo deserves.

Skip the ones you don't need. Nook won't nag you for completion.

Nook v1.0 · Snippets

The lines you keep retyping. Stash them in the corner.

Email, signature, customer reply templates — the things you'd otherwise retype a dozen times a day. Drop them into Nook's snippet rail.

⌥1 / ⌥2 / ⌥3 pastes them anywhere — terminal, mail, IM, docs. Cross-app, no friction.

Nook v1.0 · Apple Reminders sync

Also on your iPhone.

Tasks with due dates flow into Apple Reminders' "Nook" list and ride iCloud to all your devices.

Capture once on your Mac. Glance once on your iPhone in the elevator. That's it.
